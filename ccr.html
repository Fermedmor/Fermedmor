Strategic Leadership in uncertain times

As the manager of the co-curricular record at a university, I oversaw the transition to a new application during the COVID-19 pandemic. This was a critical task, as the co-curricular record is a formal recognition of a student's engagement in extracurricular activities and provides valuable evidence of the skills and experiences they have gained outside of the classroom.

Through leadership and strategic thinking, I successfully navigated this transition and ensured that the co-curricular record continued to provide students with a valuable tool for showcasing their skills to potential employers.

I worked closely with a team of developers to ensure that the new application was functional and user-friendly. I also collaborated with various stakeholders, including student groups, faculty, and staff, to gather feedback and ensure that the co-curricular record continued to accurately reflect the skills and experiences that students were gaining through their extracurricular activities.

To ensure that students were able to showcase their skills effectively, I developed and implemented training programs that educated students on how to obtain their co-curricular experiences.

As a result of these efforts, the co-curricular record continued to provide students with a valuable tool for showcasing their skills and experiences to potential employers, even during the pandemic. The new application received positive feedback from users, and it has continued to be an important tool for students' academic and career success.
